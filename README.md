# About Me:
<div style="text-align: justify;"> Hi Hi Hi  I am Dr. Shahul Hameed Chettali, a researcher in Computer Science and Engineering, awarded with a highly commendable Ph.D., with a solid academic foundation that includes a Diploma, B.E., and M.E. in Computer Science and Engineering. I am presently serving as an Assistant Professor in a reputable university. I am a formaer Project Associate II at the Centre for Aerospace and Research, Anna University, Madras Institute of Technology (MIT), Chennai, where I actively contribute to interdisciplinary research aligned with aerospace and advanced engineering applications.<br><br>My research primarily focuses on computer vision, deep learning, artificial intelligence, and applied machine learning, with particular emphasis on object detection, image understanding, and algorithmic optimization for real-world systems. I work extensively on YOLO-based architectures, visual data preprocessing, feature alignment, and performance enhancement techniques, aiming to improve detection accuracy, robustness, and interpretability in complex visual environments.<br><br>A notable outcome of my research is the development of the Sha Rotation Thumbnail (SRT) algorithm, proposed to overcome object visualization and orientation limitations observed in YOLOv5. By introducing optimized rotational alignment for detected objects, the SRT algorithm enhances thumbnail representation and practical usability of detection outputs. This contribution addresses a critical gap between theoretical detection accuracy and real-world visualization requirements, and I am currently preparing this work for publication in a reputed peer-reviewed journal.<br><br>My broader research interests include AI-driven decision support systems, intelligent image analysis, optimization of deep neural networks, and the deployment of vision-based models in constrained and applied environments. In addition to research, I actively engage in academic evaluation and mentorship, having served as a technical jury member for hackathons related to electronic communication and engineering, where I assess innovation, technical depth, and implementation feasibility.<br><br>Through my research and professional activities, I aim to bridge the gap between theoretical advancements in artificial intelligence and their practical engineering applications, contributing scalable, efficient, and impactful solutions to contemporary technological challenges.</div>
